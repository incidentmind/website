# IncidentMind first-client marketing plan

## The short version

You do **not** have a capability problem.

You have a **positioning and sales-process problem**.

Right now, IncidentMind has real technical credibility, real founder proof, and real delivery ability. But the market does not buy “AI agents,” “MCP,” or “workflow orchestration” first. It buys:

- fewer missed leads
- faster response times
- more booked jobs
- less admin work
- a clear return on money spent

That is why the best first-client wedge for IncidentMind is:

> **HVAC and home-service companies in the Raleigh-Durham area that already get inbound leads but respond too slowly or inconsistently.**

For the next 90 days, do **not** sell “custom AI automation” broadly.

Sell one narrow, simple result:

> **“We help HVAC companies respond to every lead in under 60 seconds, qualify the customer, and book more jobs automatically.”**

If you stay focused on that, you can close a first pilot much faster than if you pitch yourself as a general AI consultant.

---

## What the research says

### What IncidentMind already has going for it

Based on the live site, blog, and open-source footprint, your strengths are clear:

- strong founder credibility from real production systems and scaled businesses
- technical authority in automation, AI workflows, and integrations
- thoughtful content that shows you actually understand complex systems
- local presence in the Raleigh-Durham area

### The biggest issue to fix first

Your **live site** and your **local source copy** are telling two different stories.

The live site at `incidentmind.com` still reads like an enterprise AI automation consultancy focused on MCP, internal tools, incident workflows, and human-in-the-loop systems (see Sources 1 and 2 below).

But your local source in `src/index.11tydata.yaml` has already started repositioning toward:

- service businesses
- lead response automation
- HVAC / local operators
- booked jobs and revenue outcomes

That means your strategy is moving in the right direction, but the public market signal is still mixed.

If you try to sell HVAC owners while the live site still looks like a deep-tech consultancy, you will create friction and lose trust.

### Market signals worth using in your pitch

The outside research strongly supports a speed-to-lead offer:

- Rework summarizes MIT / HBR / InsideSales findings showing that contacting inbound leads within 5 minutes dramatically improves qualification odds, with one commonly cited figure being **21x more likely to qualify vs waiting 30 minutes** (Source 3).
- The same source states that **78% of customers buy from the first company to respond** (Source 3).
- Hatch analyzed **132,188 HVAC speed-to-lead campaigns** and found that average performance is far from best-in-class. Their top campaigns responded immediately, used multi-touch follow-up, and achieved much higher response rates than weak one-message campaigns (Source 4).
- Hatch also reports that **88% of users in its study took more than 5 minutes to reply to leads**, which shows how common the speed gap still is (Source 4).
- Go Answer cites that **over 70% of incoming HVAC leads do not convert into sales**, with many failing due to weak communication follow-through (Source 5).

### What that means for IncidentMind

This is the opportunity:

> Many HVAC companies do not need more leads first. They need to stop wasting the leads they already paid for.

That is a much easier service to sell than “AI transformation.”

## The decision: who you should target first

### Primary niche

Start with:

> **HVAC companies in Raleigh, Durham, Cary, Apex, Chapel Hill, Morrisville, Wake Forest, and nearby markets.**

### Why HVAC first

This niche works because it matches both market psychology and founder reality:

1. **The pain is obvious and expensive**  
    Missed calls, slow replies, and no follow-up directly kill revenue.

2. **The ROI is easy to explain**  
    If one extra booked job per month covers your fee, the sale becomes much simpler.

3. **The buying process is simpler than enterprise AI**  
    You are usually selling to an owner or operator, not a committee.

4. **Local trust matters**  
    Being in the Raleigh-Durham area is an advantage. Local buyers are easier to reach and easier to close.

5. **Your technical depth becomes a hidden advantage**  
    They do not need to understand how it works. They only need to believe it will answer faster and book more jobs.

### Secondary niches only after HVAC

Only expand after you have traction.

Good next options:

- plumbing
- electrical
- roofing
- restoration
- med spas / home improvement services

### What to avoid for now

Avoid these until you have case studies and a repeatable process:

- generic “small business” outreach
- enterprise AI transformation offers
- agencies as a first primary niche
- multi-niche messaging on the homepage

Too much choice kills action. That is basic Hick’s Law doing its annoying but accurate job.

## Your ideal first client profile

Target companies with most of these traits:

- 5 to 50 employees
- owner-led or ops-manager-led
- already running Google Ads, Google LSAs, Angi, Yelp, or website lead forms
- at least 20 inbound leads per month
- no dedicated 24/7 office response team
- slow or inconsistent after-hours response
- basic systems already in place like ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber, email, calendars, web forms, or call tracking

### Best-fit signals

These are green flags:

- they advertise heavily
- they have a contact form but no instant reply flow
- they mention “call now” or “24/7 service,” but their follow-up is still manual
- they have reviews mentioning slow callbacks or no response
- they service emergency or urgent categories

### Bad-fit signals

These are yellow or red flags:

- one-person shop with almost no inbound volume
- very low-ticket jobs where economics are weak
- owner wants “AI” mainly because it sounds cool
- company is disorganized enough that they cannot implement even a basic intake workflow

## The offer you should sell first

### Core offer

> **Lead Response Automation for HVAC Companies**

### Promise

> **Every new lead gets an immediate response, basic qualification, and a path to booking without waiting on a human to notice the inquiry.**

### Deliverables

Your starter offer should include:

- instant SMS or email acknowledgment within 60 seconds
- simple lead qualification flow
- routing based on service type, urgency, or location
- booking handoff or scheduling link
- after-hours response coverage
- simple reporting dashboard
- human fallback when automation cannot finish the job

### Founding-client package

For your first 2 clients only, offer a tightly scoped “founding client” version:

- setup: **$1,500–$2,500**
- monthly: **$500–$900**

In exchange, ask for:

- honest feedback
- permission to turn results into a case study
- a testimonial if results are real

### Standard package after first wins

- setup: **$2,500–$5,000**
- monthly: **$750–$1,500**

### How to position price psychologically

Use anchoring properly:

- mention that IncidentMind also builds much larger custom automation systems
- then present the HVAC starter offer as the **simple, focused version**

This makes the starter package feel like a practical entry point instead of a risky experiment.

### Risk-reversal offer

Do **not** guarantee revenue.

Instead, use a delivery-based guarantee you control:

> **“If I can’t get your first automated lead-response workflow live within 14 days of kickoff, I keep working at no extra setup cost until it is live.”**

That reduces regret aversion without making reckless promises.

## The message: what to say instead of “AI automation”

### The job to be done

Your buyers are not hiring you for AI.

They are hiring you to:

- stop wasting paid leads
- answer after-hours inquiries
- book more jobs without hiring another CSR
- follow up consistently
- look more responsive than competitors

### Your one-sentence positioning

Use this everywhere:

> **IncidentMind helps HVAC companies respond to every lead in under 60 seconds, qualify customers automatically, and book more jobs without adding office staff.**

### Messaging pillars

#### 1. Outcome, not technology

Say:

- more booked jobs
- faster replies
- fewer missed leads
- less admin work

Do not lead with:

- AI agents
- MCP
- RAG
- orchestration
- pipelines

#### 2. Loss aversion

Owners feel pain more strongly than opportunity.

Use framing like:

- “You already paid for the lead. Don’t lose it because no one replied fast enough.”
- “Every missed callback is a job your competitor might book.”

#### 3. Authority and social proof

You do not yet have HVAC case studies, so use founder credibility carefully:

- built and scaled real production systems
- worked in high-reliability environments
- shipped automation in regulated enterprise settings

Frame it as:

> “I build reliable automation that works in the real world, and now I’m applying that same discipline to lead response for service businesses.”

#### 4. Simplicity beats cleverness

Use one offer, one niche, one CTA.

That reduces decision friction for both you and the buyer.

#### 5. Foot-in-the-door

The first ask should not be “buy my system.”

The first ask should be:

> **“Want me to show you where leads are leaking from your current intake flow?”**

That is easier to say yes to.

## Website changes you should make before serious outreach

This is the single most important tactical note in this plan:

> **Do not start major HVAC outreach until the live site matches the HVAC offer.**

### Why

Right now, if an HVAC owner clicks through from your email or Loom, they may land on a site that still feels like enterprise AI consulting.

That creates a credibility gap:

- your message says “more booked jobs”
- your website says “MCP servers and enterprise AI agents”

That mismatch will quietly destroy response rates.

### Minimum site changes

Before outreach, make sure the public site has:

1. **A clear headline for service businesses**  
    Example: “Stop losing leads. Respond instantly and book more jobs automatically.”

2. **A clear niche section**  
    “Best for HVAC and local service businesses that already get inbound leads.”

3. **A starter offer section**  
    Show the lead response automation package first.

4. **A simple proof section**  
    Even if you lack client logos, show a clear before/after workflow and founder credibility.

5. **A short demo CTA**  
    “Book a free 30-minute lead response audit.”

6. **One outcome-driven contact path**  
    Demo or email. Not six equally weighted choices.

### Keep the technical proof, but move it lower

Your blog and open-source work are still valuable. They help authority.

But for HVAC buyers, they should support the sale, not lead it.

I took a quick look at {{CompanyName}} and thought there may be an opportunity to tighten up response time and after-hours follow-up.

— Viktor

### Shorter email version

Hi {{FirstName}},

Quick question: when someone fills out a form or calls after hours, how fast does your team usually respond?

I help HVAC companies answer faster, qualify leads automatically, and book more jobs from the leads they already have.

Happy to send a quick tailored demo if useful.

— Viktor

### Cold call opener

“Hey, I’ll keep this quick. I help HVAC companies respond to new leads faster and book more jobs automatically. Quick question: when someone submits a request after hours or while your team is busy, what usually happens next?”

If they answer, stay curious.

Do **not** start pitching for 5 minutes straight.

### If they say they already respond fast

Say:

“That’s great. Are you confident that’s true for nights, weekends, and missed calls too?”

### If they say they are interested but busy

Say:

“Totally fair. Want me to send a short video showing what I’d automate first for your business?”

### Voicemail

“Hey {{Name}}, this is Viktor with IncidentMind. I help HVAC companies respond to new leads faster and recover jobs that slip through slow follow-up. I’m sending a short email as well. If useful, I can show you a 3-minute walkthrough tailored to your current setup.”

### Follow-up email after no reply

Hi {{FirstName}},

Following up in case lead response speed is something you are trying to improve this season.

I’m not talking about replacing your team or adding another bloated tool. I mean a simple workflow that makes sure every new inquiry gets a fast reply and a clear next step.

If helpful, I can send a short example built around your website and lead flow.

— Viktor

## Personalized Loom strategy

This should be your highest-converting move.

### Why it works

It uses several strong psychological levers without being manipulative:

- **mere exposure:** they see your face and hear your voice
- **authority:** you sound like someone who understands systems
- **availability heuristic:** the missed-lead problem becomes vivid and concrete
- **commitment pressure stays low:** they are just watching, not buying

### Loom structure

Keep it to **3 to 5 minutes**.

1. Show their website
2. Point out where a lead enters
3. Explain what likely happens now
4. Show where delay or drop-off may happen
5. Show your simple fix
6. End with one CTA: “If you want, I can map this for your team in a 30-minute call.”

### Loom CTA line

> “If this looks useful, reply with ‘demo’ and I’ll show you what this would look like for your exact lead flow.”

That is easier than asking them to fill a form immediately.

## Your sales call process

### Goal of the first call

Not “close the deal instantly.”

The goal is:

1. confirm they have the problem
2. confirm it is expensive enough to matter
3. show that your solution is simple and practical
4. get agreement on a pilot

### Discovery questions

Ask:

- How do new leads come in today?
- What happens after business hours?
- Who owns follow-up?
- How quickly do you think most leads get a response?
- Where do leads get stuck or lost?
- Which lead sources matter most?
- What is one booked job worth on average?

### Demo structure

Use this sequence:

1. **Current state**  
    “Here’s what likely happens now.”

2. **Leakage point**  
    “Here’s where paid leads go cold.”

3. **Future state**  
    “Here’s how every lead gets answered immediately.”

4. **Human handoff**  
    “Here’s where your team stays in control.”

5. **ROI math**  
    “If this recovers even one or two jobs a month, it pays for itself.”

### Close line

> “The simplest next step would be a small pilot on your main lead source so we can prove it with minimal risk. If that works, we expand from there.”

That keeps status-quo bias from killing the deal.

## Simple ROI model for conversations

Do not overcomplicate this.

Use a back-of-the-envelope model like:

- 100 leads per month
- current weak response / follow-up means too many go cold
- recovering even 5 extra conversations can lead to 1–3 extra booked jobs
- if an average job is worth $500 to $2,000+, the economics are easy to see

### Example talk track

“Let’s say you’re already paying for 100 leads a month. If slow response or missed follow-up costs you even a few real jobs, that can be thousands in lost revenue. My job is to help you recover value from leads you already paid for.”

Keep it simple. Owners do not want spreadsheet theater.

## What to do if HVAC does not convert quickly

If you do **30 days of focused outreach** and get strong conversations but no close, do not panic-pivot after five days.

Review:

- Is the live site still mismatched?
- Is the offer too vague?
- Is the price too high for a first pilot?
- Are you contacting buyers with real lead volume?
- Are your Looms specific enough?

If the answer to those is solid and HVAC still feels slow, expand in this order:

1. plumbing
2. electrical
3. roofing

Only return to broader B2B technical automation if you decide you want a longer, more consultative sales motion that better matches the current blog and enterprise proof.

If I were prioritizing this for IncidentMind right now, I would do exactly this:

1. **Commit to HVAC / local service positioning for 90 days**
